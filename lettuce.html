7 Tips on how to grow our own green leaf Garden at home

  1. Finding the right place

    2. The first step which is important as we want to grow our green garden is to find the right place. Most greens and vegetables grow where there is enough sunlight. Keep your place as much as possible away from strogn wind spots in your area as it will not let your greens have an on time grow.Stay far away from trees; a dense leaf canopy casts too much shade, and thirsty tree roots quickly grow into veggie-garden soil and out-compete your crops. The best season for sowing starts between May and June for most of the vegetables and green leafs/Herbs planting times.

  2. Plant in Pots or Blocks

    3. It is preferable to grow vegetables and green leaf plants for consumption in soil lengthwise to have a better contribution for the green bed, in order to handle and take care of them more easy going and have a better sunlight covered surface. A better idea, especially on a small plot, is to plant in blocks with the same spacing in all directions. Stay on the paths between the beds and avoid compacting the soil. This virtually eliminates the need for tilling and heavy digging. If you use organic matter to add extra depth, work it in to improve the soil at the same time and keep the soil wet 55%-70% of the time..

  3. Choose the right Variety

    4. It hasn't been more easy to choose lettuce variety for our garden! Keep in mind that all the green lettuces have similar consistant and the value to our table is alsmost the same. There are many varieties available online or at the store which some of them are:

    • Arugula
    • Butterhead lettuce
    • Coral lettuce
    • Little gem lettuce
    • Red sails lettuce
    • Red leaf
    • Romaine
    • Mesclun
    • Green leaf
    • Iceberg

  4. Watering your garden

    5. Water young seedlings and newly transplanted lettuces regularly, especially in warm weather. To get the best results, lettuces will prefer a fertile soil, ideally one that was improved with well-rotted organic matter last autumn.

  5. Let your self make mistakes to learn from them

    6. One more important but not neccecary step is to let our selfs to make mistakes as we learn from them in order to become better on what we are doing. Heat stress and bolting can occur in plants that are not watered frequently enough. Such crops are no longer marketable. Overwatering is also undesirable because of the increased risk of root rot and fungal diseases. Some experience needed after our first attmept on sowing lettuces and vegetables. Don't be afraid of them.

  6. Be aware of bugs and pests

    7. Keep plants as healthy as possible by supplying good soil, adequate moisture, and nutrition to maximize plant immune systems. Strong, healthy plants fight off bug and disease pressure readily. Monitor regularly for early signs of trouble, but donâ€™t just spray the whole garden at the first sign of something crawling.It's always a more safe way to ask at the garden store for the right bug control preventer as each case is different.

  7. How long does it lasts from start to end?

    8. The total time in days is 40 to 60 days after planting. After about three weeks from sowing you will need to thin your plants to 5cm (2 inches) apart. Then from 5 weeks after sowing you will be able to remove further young leaves and progressively thin out your plants. Without taking the risk to wait longer thatn that, most lettuces will be ready to harvest as a whole just 10 weeks after sowing. It is always in your hand to harvest when you need it, even is if is not evenly grown. Your patience will pay you off after these weeks.
